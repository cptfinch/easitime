# Financial Sustainability Model

## Goal
Can a timebank (or a timebanking platform) ever be financially self-sustaining? Model different scenarios.

## Scenarios

### 1. Grant-dependent (status quo)
- Annual coordinator cost: £20-50k
- Funding: lottery, local authority, NHS grants (1-3 year terms)
- Question: What's the survival probability over 5/10 years?

### 2. Membership fees
- At what annual fee level is it sustainable?
- What's the impact on participation (price elasticity)?
- Tiered: free for low-income, paid for others?

### 3. NHS social prescribing funding
- Per-referral payment from ICB/PCN
- Annual outcomes-based contract
- What volume of referrals needed to cover coordinator?

### 4. Platform SaaS model (Adjoint)
- Freemium: free <50 members, paid tiers above
- NHS/LA enterprise licensing
- Fiat bridge revenue
- What's the breakeven number of paying communities?

### 5. Hybrid
- Part-funded coordinator + volunteer co-coordinators
- Mixed revenue: some grants + some fees + some NHS funding
- Most realistic scenario

## Tools
- Python: NumPy, Monte Carlo simulation
- Spreadsheet modelling for simple scenarios
