# Care Time Bank — "Bank Time for Future Care"

## Concept
Based on the Swiss **Zeitvorsorge** (time savings) model from St. Gallen:
- Members aged 50+ contribute care hours to other community members
- Hours are "banked" in their personal account
- When the contributing member themselves needs care, they draw down their banked hours
- A form of intergenerational social insurance, outside the money economy

## How It Works

### Depositing Time
1. Member provides care/support to another member (shopping, companionship, gardening, transport)
2. Both parties confirm the exchange
3. Hours are credited to the provider's "care savings account"

### Withdrawing Time
1. Member develops care needs (mobility issues, illness, loneliness)
2. They request services from their banked hours
3. Other members provide the care
4. Hours are debited from the requester's account

### Key Differences from Standard Timebanking
- **Long-term savings**: Credits don't expire (or have very long expiry, e.g., 10 years)
- **Aging focus**: Designed specifically for later-life care needs
- **Intergenerational**: Younger members bank time they'll use decades later
- **Supplementary**: Complements, doesn't replace, formal social care

## Evidence Base
- **Zeitvorsorge St. Gallen**: 320 members, 80,000+ hours banked, operational since 2012
- **Hong Kong quasi-experimental studies**: Timebanking improves volunteering, empowerment, and QoL in older adults (2025)
- **WHO recognition**: Timebanking as transformative approach for active aging
- **Fureai Kippu (Japan)**: Elder care time credits since 1973, 302 branches
